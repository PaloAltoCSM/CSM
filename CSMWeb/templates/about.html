Introduction

The effectiveness of many complex systems involves common aspects such as energy utilization, information processing, memory, perception, learning, environmental interaction, coordinated organization of subsystems, efficient use of scarce resources, etc. As these systems evolve, typically in rich ecosystems of other complex systems, their structure grows to reflect the structure and patterns of their environment, either through learned modes of behavior, or even though explicit representation and models of the environment. The evolution of these systems proceeds at many time scales and levels of organization, and ranging from slow continuous adaptation, to qualitative change of structure (e.g emergence of new species) to even new levels of organizations (e.g. development of increasingly synergistic communities which effectively become new levels of organism.) Collectively the process taking place in these systems, and in the overall evolving ecosystem could be thought of as intelligence in a general sense.

The above description presents the general area of interest of CSM, but leaves out one fundamental concept - namely mind. Indeed as a result of the European Enlightenment and rise of Cartesian duality, a tendency of modern science has been to deny mind altogether (particularly for systems other than human beings) or to deny it as a legitimate domain for scientific understanding. When it has been studied, it is often reduced to the study of objective descriptions that fail to capture the very starting point of human understanding - which is our subjective experience. We learn to believe in and treat an ‘objective world’ as primary, and consider our subjective experience as derived, or as some emergent phenomenon, or deny it as worthy of study. In truth, however, each of us knows only one thing - that we ourselves have some kind of sense of experience.

The Center for Study of Mind seeks to bridge between the objective description of general intelligent systems - the modality of science, and the understanding of mind through our subjective experience and consciousness. Our goal is not only to further the scientific understanding of those systems, but to integrate that understanding with our subjective experience, and so enrich our experience and better understand our place in the evolution of the cosmos.

Mission

To help connect people by making it easier to provide, find, organize and share views of the world.  

 

This involves providing access to technologies that allow us to see other parts of the world, and to share our own, in many exciting ways, such as live or recorded images, video, and virtual models. More importantly, it involves using those technologies to help us connect, and to share our stories, concerns and dreams and aspirations. {% endblock %}
